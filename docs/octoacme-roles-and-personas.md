# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers own the long-term product vision, strategy, and roadmap. They focus on market positioning, competitive analysis, and ensuring the product meets broader business objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Define product vision, strategy, and long-term roadmap
- Conduct market research and competitive analysis
- Set high-level success metrics and business objectives
- Collaborate with Product Owner on feature prioritization
- Present product strategy to executive stakeholders

### Goals
- Maximize market fit and business value
- Guide product evolution based on market trends
- Ensure alignment between product strategy and business goals

### Typical Communication
- Monthly/quarterly roadmap reviews with executives
- Market insights and competitive analysis reports
- Strategic alignment with Product Owner and Project Manager

### Key Interactions
- **Product Owner**: Aligns on strategic priorities and feature definitions
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates on timeline feasibility and resource constraints
- **Developers**: Provides strategic context for technical decisions

---

## Product Owner

### Role Summary
Product Owner defines and prioritizes specific product features for upcoming sprints and releases. They serve as the primary liaison between stakeholders and the development team, ensuring features deliver customer value.

### Responsibilities
- Define and prioritize product backlog items and features
- Write clear user stories with acceptance criteria
- Interface with stakeholders to gather requirements and feedback
- Validate completed features against acceptance criteria
- Make tactical prioritization decisions during sprints
- Collaborate with UX Designer on user experience requirements

### Goals
- Deliver high-value features that meet customer needs
- Maintain a healthy, prioritized backlog
- Ensure team works on the right things at the right time

### Typical Communication
- Daily/weekly backlog refinement sessions
- Sprint planning and review meetings
- Feature specifications and user stories
- Stakeholder demos and feedback sessions

### Key Interactions
- **Product Manager**: Aligns tactical priorities with strategic vision
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates on scheduling and resource allocation
- **Developers**: Clarifies requirements and acceptance criteria
- **Quality Assurance Lead**: Defines quality expectations and test scenarios
- **UX Designer**: Collaborates on user experience and interface design
- **Business Analyst**: Refines requirements and validates business logic

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Key Interactions
- **Product Manager**: Ensures timeline feasibility for strategic initiatives
- **Product Owner**: Coordinates sprint planning and capacity management
- **Quality Assurance Lead**: Tracks testing progress and quality gates
- **Developers**: Monitors progress and removes impediments
- **Business Analyst**: Aligns on requirements documentation and scope

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations
- Collaborate with UX Designer on implementing user interfaces

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Key Interactions
- **Product Owner**: Clarifies requirements and acceptance criteria
- **Quality Assurance Lead**: Collaborates on test strategies and bug fixes
- **UX Designer**: Implements designs and provides feasibility feedback
- **Project Manager**: Reports progress and escalates blockers
- **Business Analyst**: Validates technical implementation against business rules

---

## Quality Assurance Lead

### Role Summary
Quality Assurance Lead oversees all testing activities and ensures that acceptance criteria and quality standards are met before features are released. They establish testing strategies and quality gates throughout the development lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain testing strategies and quality standards
- Oversee test planning, execution, and reporting
- Ensure acceptance criteria are testable and verified
- Coordinate automated and manual testing efforts
- Track and prioritize defects with the team
- Validate releases meet quality gates before deployment

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Maintain high product quality and reliability
- Enable fast, confident releases through comprehensive testing

### Typical Communication
- Daily test status updates in standups
- Weekly quality metrics and defect reports
- Test plans and test case documentation
- Release readiness assessments

### Key Interactions
- **Product Owner**: Ensures acceptance criteria are clear and testable
- **Developers**: Collaborates on test strategies, reviews PRs for quality
- **Project Manager**: Reports on testing progress and quality risks
- **UX Designer**: Validates user experience meets design specifications
- **Business Analyst**: Verifies test scenarios cover business requirements

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designer designs user interfaces, conducts user research, and applies user-centric design principles to ensure the product is intuitive, accessible, and delightful to use.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Define user flows and information architecture
- Establish and maintain design systems and UI patterns
- Ensure accessibility standards are met (WCAG compliance)
- Validate implemented features match design specifications

### Goals
- Create intuitive, user-friendly interfaces
- Improve user satisfaction and engagement
- Ensure consistent user experience across the product

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- User research findings and personas
- Design specifications and style guides
- Usability testing reports

### Key Interactions
- **Product Owner**: Collaborates on feature definitions and user requirements
- **Developers**: Provides design specifications and implementation guidance
- **Quality Assurance Lead**: Validates UI/UX in testing
- **Business Analyst**: Ensures designs support business workflows
- **Product Manager**: Aligns design direction with product strategy

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analyst gathers and analyzes requirements, serving as a bridge between business stakeholders and technical teams. They ensure that business needs are accurately captured and translated into actionable technical requirements.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Analyze current processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Create detailed functional specifications and user stories
- Facilitate requirements workshops and validation sessions
- Maintain requirements traceability throughout the project
- Validate solutions meet business objectives

### Goals
- Ensure clear understanding of business needs across all teams
- Reduce rework by clarifying requirements early
- Maximize business value from technical implementations

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and specifications
- Process flow diagrams and business rules
- Stakeholder interview notes and workshop summaries
- Requirements validation sessions

### Key Interactions
- **Product Owner**: Refines and clarifies feature requirements
- **Developers**: Translates business requirements into technical specifications
- **Quality Assurance Lead**: Defines test scenarios based on business rules
- **UX Designer**: Ensures designs support business workflows
- **Project Manager**: Provides input on scope and requirements changes
